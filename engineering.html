Engineering and Advanced Manufacturing

All our honors classes count as AP

Honors PLTW Intro To Engineering Design

Ms Adams & Mr Wycoff

Student course work revolves around the design process. They work individually or together, using 3D modeling software and an engineering notebook to document their projects and work. Students will also used applied math and science.

Honors PLTW Principles of Engineering

Mr Jackson

POE is a very project based class and after learning how to use the design process and how to think like an engineer, students tackle on a variety of engineering topics. They learn problem solving skills and leadership skills.

Honors PLTW Computer Integrated Manufacturing

Mr Wycoff

Students will shift towards a more robotics engineering and understand more about product design. Students will learn more about the innovative nature of modern manufacturing.

Honors PLTW Digital Electronics

Ms Alina

Students will learn about the world of electronics around them and understand the process of creating today's world technology. This classed is focused on circuit design and learning topics such as combinational and logic gates.

Honors PLTW Engineering Design and Development

Ms Alina

All of the skills engineering has taught students will be uses as they challenge their design capabilities. They use all steps of the design process to define a real world problem and find a solution.

Advanced Manufacturing I

Mr Lovell

Meant to be a pre-engineering course, students get a glimpse of engineering and the design process, as well as advancements in industry and trade.

Advanced Manufacturing II

Mr Lovell

Advanced manufacturing II uses prior knowledge from the first course. Students, using what they know, take the next level of manufacturing and learn more about complex manufacturing and global economy.
